The Hypothesis

Most financial news is written for experts — leaving everyday people feeling confused or excluded. I hypothesised that a simple translation layer would resonate with a large, underserved audience.

Target: ages 25–44, financially curious but not expert. No prior investing knowledge assumed.

Key Findings

  • 1.75% CTR — roughly 2× the Facebook benchmark of ~0.9%, indicating strong message-market fit
  • €0.81 CPC from a cold audience with zero brand recognition
  • 9,162 unique people reached on a €129 budget — lean and efficient
  • Comparison creative (Traditional News vs. Whale) drove the highest engagement
  • Low conversion post-click suggests the landing page offer was too weak — the ad created curiosity but the page didn't close it

Outcome & Next Steps

What the data showed: A 1.75% CTR — nearly double the industry average — confirmed that the problem resonates. People saw the ad and clicked, meaning the pain point (jargon-heavy financial news) is real and the messaging landed.

Key learning: Despite strong ad performance, conversion on the landing page was low. The bottleneck wasn't demand — it was the offer itself. The page failed to clearly communicate what users would get or why they should sign up now. Click intent was there; the landing page didn't capitalise on it.

Decision made: Validated that the problem is worth solving. Next step is to redesign the landing page with a stronger, more specific offer — and rerun the experiment to isolate whether conversion improves with ad copy held constant.
